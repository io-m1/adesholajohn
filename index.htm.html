Link 01 — Profile

From the tower up


For over a decade, I've been the person operators send up the tower when a transmission link needs to go in, come down, or come back online. Since November 2015 I've worked microwave transmission for contracting outfits based in Accra — Primus Engineering / AdminTelecom, and GoodMast Telecom / Aviat Networks — climbing towers, monopoles and rooftops to install, align and commission the microwave backbone that carries voice and data traffic across Ghana's mobile networks, principally on the MTN Ghana network.

"Every job closed the same way — tested, documented, and handed back to the NOC and project team with a clear, well-detailed report."

My work covers the full lifecycle of a transmission site: civil works for tower erection, GSM antenna and RRU installation, Ethernet / coaxial / fiber connectivity between nodes, -48V DC power systems, and the switching and routing configuration that brings a link into service on both Huawei and Aviat Networks equipment. I've also handled the higher-stakes end — equipment swap/change-out, traffic re-routing, and live cutover/migration during RAN modernization upgrades.

Since 2025 I've been on active field duty under Afro Egypt Engineering, deployed as a subcontractor to Huawei Technology Ghana Ltd in support of the Telecel Ghana network. Day-to-day work includes microwave link installation and alignment on Huawei transmission equipment, BTS and RRU commissioning, fiber and coaxial integration, and fault diagnosis and resolution on live operator sites across Greater Accra and beyond.
